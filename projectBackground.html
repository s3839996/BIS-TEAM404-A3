PROJECT BACKGROUND


MOTIVATION

The original motivation for this project came from the discovery of a project known as the ‘Android Parking Booking Project’. The main goal of this project was to give users a way to find and reserve parking spaces ahead of time so that did not need to search for one when they arrived at their destination. Seeing the advanced criteria and usefulness of this project we thought it’d be great to not only expand on this idea but popularise it as it could be very helpful with day-to-day tasks, as well as give us a challenge to improve our IT abilities.
Being road users ourselves, we understand the frustration that comes with finding a parking space, so this would be a great investment to reduce the number of cars driving around looking for somewhere to park, especially during peak hour. We know what it is like having to drive around looking for somewhere to park, and what it is using apps that are not the most user friendly. Therefore, we are in a good position to build a helpful and efficient app for users who drive often.
By getting a start on this project now, despite the COVID-19 pandemic being a current worldwide issue, we would be getting ahead of any other possible competitors. Having an application like this, especially when people start going out more often. This would be a great opportunity to get ahead and become popular with the consumers who find it difficult to find a parking spot. We also stress that our goal is not to copy the original project, but rather expand upon the idea and include our own personal features that we feel would benefit.

    SCOPE

    The scope of our project was to have a somewhat functional application of our idea, ‘Quick Park.’ We planned to at least deliver an idea of this through some form like a prototype or even just small mock-ups of what we believe our application would look like in the final stages. We had hope to gain the knowledge of how to go about the production of the application throughout this course, but our plans changed once we understood that we would have to likely learn this without much guidance from a lecturer or tutor. Early on, we decided to research and get some ideas on how to develop an application. This helped us get an idea on the possibilities of what we could make, as such our scope planned to have a functioning prototype, so with the minimal viable features in tact as much as we feel we can accomplish. We also planned to have this usable on a mobile device, which would greatly help our idea of making the application feel convenient and easy to use.
