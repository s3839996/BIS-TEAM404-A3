PROJECT PROGRESS


DESCRIPTION

Quick Park began in 2020 when Team 404 wanted to make it easier for people to find parking, especially in the CBD where there is lots of traffic. The development of the 'Quick Park' application will assist users to conveniently park their vehicles amidst all the hustle bustle within the city. Users are required to install the application, register their vehicle, enter their card details, location and get started. During the initial analysis stages the team created descriptions to outline what the app would do, including each feature. We also created architecture diagrams such as IPO charts, and UCD charts, and more which were going to be used to show how the system works.

During the development of the app we discovered that due to our limited knowledge of app development we would need to change from an actual app. We decided that the next best option for the app would be to create a mobile website. Using our knowledge of PHP, HTML, and CSS we created a website for the app. We also needed to downscale some of our features to make them more reasonable. For example, for the view bookings feature we couldn’t get it to view the bookings from the JSON files, therefore we could not develop that feature. Another example would be the directions/live tracking feature which we had to develop with limited functionality. Currently the user cannot select a destination, but instead it only shows a fixed location to show that the feature works.

An example of a feature that was completed is the directions to the destination in the live tracking feature. Under the interactive map in the Quick Park app, the user can read the step by step directions to their destination.

Overall, our app did not differ too much for our original plan, even though we needed to make the app into a mobile website, the app still functions similarly to what we originally thought.

One of our biggest issues that we had was time. Due to our time constraint we did not have the time to fully develop the application. Due to this we had to scale back some features which we mentioned above.


OUTCOMES TO DATE

As of the project completion, we’ve been able to get all of our minimum viable features completed. Whilst they may not all be completed to their fullest extent as we had originally planned, we were still able to get majority of their functionality working in the limited and short time that we had to actually develop the program. In our presentation, we will be able to demonstrate the process of a user registering and signing into the app. Contacting the developers of the app to provide feedback, request an enquiry or talk over the phone with the provided phone number. The user will be able to make a quick booking where they can enter their location of choice, as well as the date and time they’d like to park there, and selecting the vehicle they will be driving and their selected payment method. Along with this, they can specifically select the space they’d like to park in. The user is also able to view their profile, they can edit certain details, and manage their payment settings. Finally, a live tracking option is available. This depicts what could happen if the app had a fully fledged GPS option built into it that can guide them to their selected location of parking.

SCOPE CREEP

There are aspects of the app that we weren’t able to complete due to the time constraints or resources that we had available to us. We originally planned to develop the app in Java, however we only had a short couple of weeks to do real development so we decided to create a representation of the app using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and PHP. We also intended to include the integration of the user’s phone voice assistant so that they could use it to book a parking space however this was not possible due to the time constraint we had and that ultimately it was not feasible to be able to do this. If we could repeat this project, we would have less functionality and focus on a few main features that we could really put all of our effort into.


WINDFALL

The original plan was to develop our app using the Java programming language. However, we eventually discovered that we only had three weeks to develop the final product. It was eventually decided to develop the app using HTML and CSS. By developing the app using HTML and CSS we were able to significantly reduce the amount of time that we had originally foreseen to develop the app. The benefit of HTML is that we could save templates and various design aspects and replicate this multiple times to create different pages of the app. Another advantage of HTML is that we were also able to implement the PHP language which meant data storage was a lot easier than easily anticipated, and due to our experience with PHP in Web Programming, we were able to transfer the skills over to our assignment.


PROGRESS

Based on the estimation plan from Assignment 2, the estimated average time of development was 77 hours. The total number of hours in a week is 168 meaning that the development of the project would take less than a week's effort of work. Now, we weren’t working on the app 24/7 meaning that we wouldn’t only be developing in a week and rather these hours would be spread out over several days and weeks. Not to mention that not everyone is developing at the same time so that adds more constraints to the time limit. In the three weeks we had to develop the app I’d say we spent less than 77 hours developing the app. This is due to the fact that our method of development changed from Java to HTML and most of us were already familiar with the HTML concepts needed to develop the app so that reduced the amount of learning, research and planning needed to develop the app. Additionally, the use of PHP allowed us to save templates for the site which reduced the need for coding and also made the development process more efficient.


TESTING

We have conducted many tests on our final MVF product. Whilst we had to ‘downgrade’ our features as to run on schedule, we did our best in ensuring that they fulfilled the features we did manage to implement. Field validation was a common occurring test we conducted, with the all required fields having to be filled in (such as can be seen in the contact forms, quick bookings and payment forms). Other tests with these forms included storing data in a JSON file. Though this was more for proof of concept, as this data isn’t being used for anything. We used a mixture of HTML and PHP Server Side validation for the forms. We developed functions in php to handle the read and write to JSON files, and we made functions to handle to registration, log in and logging out of the user.

EXTENDED VIABLE FEATURES


INTEGRATION FOR BUSINESSES

DESCRIPTION

A new feature that could be built into the app is business integration. This would allow business managers to create spending accounts for their employees. Users would be able to separate their transactions, and businesses would be able to immediately view their employee's receipts. For example, if an employee needed to drive to the city for a business meeting, they could put it under the business account. This would charge the businesses credit card instead of the employees personal account and the business manager would be able to view that transaction to make sure that it was approved.


USER STORIES

“Being the CEO of Uber, I get a lot of complaints from the uber drivers about not being able to find convenient safe parking nearby the pick-up or drop off locations, therefore want to be able to provide them an option to find convenient parking.”

The Quick Park application can be integrated into applications such as Uber to provide Uber drivers with a quick and effortless option of finding a parking location close to their client's pickup or drop off location. Having a guaranteed car park available will indeed support businesses like uber extend their services and increase safety precautions when it comes to uber taxi service. Once the integration for businesses occurs the uber drivers will be able to see the similar app interface to regular Quick Park users, whereby they can see the short-term car parks available nearby the location of choice. The drivers can then have the choice to book the most convenient car park closest to the client's pick-up or drop off location and can indicate to the client that their ride is ready at this location and time. The Quick Park integration for businesses can work hand in hand to support the business increase their services and boost sales.


“As the manager of a busy accounting firm in the city, I would like to provide my staff members a facility to find parking effortlessly nearby the firm.”

'Quick Park' can increase its clientele by offering service to businesses at a reasonable rate. Firms that do not have car parking available for staff will need to find parking available for staff members therefore they can ensure their employees attend to work on time. In the case of the accounting firm, the manager can use the Quick Park application to book the car parking slots for a short period and be able to get it at competitive prices. This can entail increase business relationship between Quick Park and other organisations, as parking service is managed effortlessly.

INTEGRATION INTO MAPPING SERVICE APPS

DESCRIPTION

Integrating the Quick Park system into a mapping service app such as Google Maps or Apple Maps would make users trips more efficient as they would have the ability to link their Quick Park accounts to a mapping service app. By linking their accounts users would have the option to automatically book an available parking spot nearest to their destination all within the same app. Users would not need to use their phone while driving to try and find a parking spot instead they could make the booking as they entered their destination into the app or they could book it during their journey by talking to Googles smart assistant or Apples Siri which are both integrated into their respective apps.

By allowing users to speak to their smart assistant while driving we are not only making it simpler for people to find a parking location but also creating a safer environment where people have one less reason to go on their phones while driving.


USER STORIES

“Being a new resident in Melbourne, I would like to quickly navigate through the city and be able to find secure locations to park my vehicle.”

Having the Quick Park application integrated into mapping services will allow for users, especially novice users easily navigate through Melbourne and find quick parking quickly and conveniently. The application can be integrated in a way that the user does not have to take much effort to switch between the two applications (map and the quick park application). Users will be prompted to see the car parks available on the way or nearby the location entered by the user. This will increase confidence in drivers and help them save time and energy trying to find a parking lot.

PRICE COMPARISION

DESCRIPTION

Parking in the city can become very costly even for just a few hours. Quick Park could help solve this issue through a price comparison feature. This feature would show various parking locations in a specified area in order to allow its user to find the best parking spot for them. A user might be willing to spend more money to park closer to where they want to go, or maybe the user wants to save a few dollars and instead walk. That is what this feature would allow its users to choose.


USER STORIES

“Being a member of a medium budget family, I would like to compare parking prices of nearby carparks and get the best deal.”

Displaying market prices can be beneficial for users who are on a tight budget. Quick Park can offer exclusive deals and ensure that their prices are competitive, thereby encouraging a greater pool of individuals to utilise the service. Users having the option to see the other competitor business prices and then compare to Quick Park’s reasonable prices and service will help users to make the decision on which organisation to support. Quick Park can aim to match or beat other organisation’s prices and be on top of providing excellent service at affordable prices to all kinds of users.


“As a student I would like to be able to compare and receive the cheapest bargain available to find a quick and affordable carpark.”

Quick Park can aim to support students and teenagers in finding affordable parking near the location of their choice. The price comparison feature will come in handy as these group on individuals typically are low on budget and would like to receive the lowest possible price point. Hence displaying all the prices, available and then highlighting the current rates that are offered by Quick Park can increase loyalty from clients to the business. In future Quick Park can aim to provide booster packages for students that enable them to access affordable parking effortlessly.
